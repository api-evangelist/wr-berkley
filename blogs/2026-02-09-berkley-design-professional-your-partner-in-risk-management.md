The Situation When an alleged error or omission occurs, a design firm can quickly become entangled in costly disputes or litigation—impacting finances, time, and reputation. Consider this real-world example: An architect designed a new community center for a nonprofit organization and also provided engineering services. During construction, significant quality issues arose, particularly with the foundation. The architect used its standard contract which stated they would “ensure” contractor performance per plans and specifications. Compounding the problem, the architect lacked formal written contracts with its structural engineer and geotechnical firm—only proposals existed. As a result, the architect was unable to engage its consultants in dispute resolution. The Resolution To resolve the claim, Berkley Design Professional paid in excess of $600,000 on behalf of the policyholder and nearly $100,000 in legal expenses. Lessons Learned A design professional should: never agree to “ensure” the quality of construction work—this creates an uninsurable warranty of another party’s performance. always have written contracts with subconsultants, including indemnification provisions for negligent performance. align dispute resolution processes and venues in subconsultant agreements with those in the prime agreement. Why Professional Liability Insurance Coverage Matters Professional liability (E&O) insurance isn’t just about responding to claims—it’s about preventing them. It provides the protection design professionals need against financial risks, damages, and legal costs that can arise from professional services.
